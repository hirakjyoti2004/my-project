BIRDS ENCYCLOPEDIA

Birds play crucial roles in both natural ecosystems and human societies for several reasons:
1.Pollination: Many bird species, such as hummingbirds and sunbirds, are important pollinators for various plants, including wildflowers, fruits, and crops. They help in the reproduction of plants, ensuring genetic diversity and ecosystem stability.
2.Seed dispersal:Birds aid in seed dispersal by consuming fruits and then excreting seeds in different locations, helping plants colonize new areas and maintain healthy populations.
3.Pest control: Birds such as owls, hawks, and falcons help control insect and rodent populations by preying on them, thus minimizing crop damage and reducing the need for chemical pesticides.
4.Indicator species: Birds are sensitive to changes in their environment, making them excellent indicators of ecosystem health. Declines in bird populations can signal environmental degradation or pollution.
5.Economic value: Birds contribute to economies through ecotourism, birdwatching, and recreational activities. Many people travel to observe birds in their natural habitats, generating revenue for local communities.
6.Cultural significance: Birds have been revered and celebrated in various cultures throughout history, often symbolizing freedom, beauty, and spirituality. They inspire art, literature, and folklore, enriching human culture.
7.Ecosystem balance: Birds play essential roles in food webs, both as predators and prey. Their presence helps maintain ecological balance by regulating populations of other organisms and contributing to overall biodiversity.
Overall, birds are integral components of ecosystems and provide numerous benefits to both nature and human societies, making their conservation and protection crucial for the well-being of the planet.
There are estimated to be around 10,000 bird species worldwide. Birds are characterized by feathers, a beak with no teeth, laying hard-shelled eggs, and having a high metabolic rate and a strong yet lightweight skeleton. They exhibit a remarkable diversity in size, color, habitat, and behavior, ranging from tiny hummingbirds to large ostriches, and from tropical parrots to Arctic terns.

SKYLARK (ভৰতপক্ষী)

## The skylark (Alauda arvensis) is a small bird known for its melodious song, often heard during its soaring flight. Found in open grasslands and agricultural areas, the skylark is known for its brownish plumage with streaks, and it nests on the ground. Skylarks are widespread in Europe and Asia, and their ascending song is a characteristic feature, especially during the breeding season. They feed on seeds and insects, and despite facing habitat challenges, skylarks remain an iconic bird with a distinctive and uplifting song.

SCIENTIFIC NAME:Alauda arvensis
COMMON NAME: Eurisian skylark
SIZE : 18-19cm.

##The Great Hornbill, also known as the Buceros bicornis, is a large bird native to the forests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in India, Nepal, and Southeast Asian countries. Great Hornbills are monogamous and often seen in pairs or small family groups. They face threats due to habitat loss and hunting but are also protected in many areas.

ASIAN KOEL (কুলি)

##The Asian Koel, scientifically known as Eudynamys scolopaceus, is a species of cuckoo found across South and Southeast Asia. The male is glossy bluish-black with a striking red eye, while the female is brown with white and buff streaks. They are known for their loud and distinctive calls, often heard during the breeding season. The Asian Koel primarily feeds on fruits and insects and is often found in wooded habitats, gardens, and urban areas. They are brood parasites, laying their eggs in the nests of other bird species, notably crows and mynas.
